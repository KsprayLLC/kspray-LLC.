Science & history

Benefits & History

Ketamine has decades of clinical history and a growing body of evidence supporting its use in treating some of the most difficult-to-treat conditions in modern medicine.

Therapeutic benefits

Clinically recognized positive effects

🧠

Rapid antidepressant effect

Ketamine can produce measurable antidepressant effects within hours — significantly faster than traditional antidepressants which take weeks.

💛

Treatment-resistant depression

Studies show ketamine is effective in up to 70% of patients who have not responded to two or more conventional antidepressant treatments.

🛡️

Suicidal ideation reduction

Research demonstrates that ketamine rapidly reduces suicidal thoughts, making it valuable in acute psychiatric intervention settings.

😮‍💨

Anxiety & PTSD relief

Ketamine disrupts the consolidation of fear memories, showing promise in treating PTSD and generalized anxiety disorder.

🔥

Chronic pain management

As an NMDA receptor antagonist, ketamine reduces central sensitization and has been used successfully in complex regional pain syndrome and fibromyalgia.

🔄

Neuroplasticity promotion

Ketamine stimulates the growth of new synaptic connections in the prefrontal cortex, helping the brain "reset" negative thought patterns at a structural level.

💊

Non-opioid pain relief

Ketamine provides powerful analgesia without the addiction risk profile of opioid medications, making it an important tool in the opioid crisis landscape.

🌙

OCD symptom reduction

Early clinical trials suggest that ketamine infusion can significantly reduce obsessive-compulsive symptoms within days of treatment.

⚡

Bipolar depression

Ketamine has demonstrated effectiveness in treating the depressive phase of bipolar disorder, often with fewer mood-destabilizing risks than traditional antidepressants.

Note: The benefits listed above are based on published clinical research and are provided for educational purposes only. Individual results vary. Ketamine is a prescription medication and its use should always be supervised by a licensed physician. Speak with your provider about whether ketamine nasal spray is appropriate for your specific condition.
Medical history

Ketamine's history in medicine

Far from a new drug, ketamine has a well-established, 60-year medical track record that makes it one of the most studied anesthetics in the world.

1962

First synthesized

Ketamine is first synthesized by chemist Calvin Stevens at Parke-Davis pharmaceutical company as a safer alternative to phencyclidine (PCP) for anesthesia.

1964

First human trials

Early clinical trials in human volunteers demonstrate ketamine's unique "dissociative anesthesia" — patients remain in a trance-like state while pain is blocked.

1970

FDA approval as anesthetic

The FDA approves ketamine (brand name Ketalar) for use as a general anesthetic. It is widely deployed by U.S. military medics during the Vietnam War for field surgeries.

1990s

Antidepressant research begins

Researchers begin studying ketamine's effects on the glutamate system and observe remarkable, rapid antidepressant effects in animal models, sparking a new wave of psychiatric research.

2000

Landmark clinical study

Yale University researchers publish a groundbreaking study showing that a single low dose of ketamine produces rapid, robust antidepressant effects in patients with treatment-resistant depression.

2006

NIMH confirms rapid effects

The National Institute of Mental Health confirms in a double-blind trial that ketamine relieves depression symptoms within hours — an unprecedented finding in the field.

2019

Esketamine nasal spray approved

The FDA approves Spravato® (esketamine), a nasal spray derived from ketamine, for treatment-resistant depression — marking the first genuinely new antidepressant mechanism approved in over 30 years.

Today

Expanding access through telemedicine

Compounded ketamine nasal spray, prescribed by licensed physicians via telehealth platforms, is making this therapy more accessible to patients across the country who may not have a ketamine clinic nearby.
